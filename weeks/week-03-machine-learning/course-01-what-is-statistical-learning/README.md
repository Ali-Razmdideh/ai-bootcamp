# Course 1 — What is Statistical Learning?

**Duration:** 1.5 hours  
**Slides:** [slides/index.html](slides/index.html)

## Overview

This course introduces statistical learning from the ground up, drawing
directly from Hastie & Tibshirani's *Introduction to Statistical Learning*.

**Part 1 — Introduction** covers why the field matters (Watson, Nate Silver,
Hal Varian), eight canonical learning problems (prostate cancer, phonemes,
spam, zip codes, cancer genes, salary, LANDSAT), the taxonomy of supervised /
unsupervised / semi-supervised learning, and the Netflix Prize as a case study.

**Part 2 — What is Statistical Learning?** covers the formal framework:
Y = f(X) + ε, the regression function as the ideal predictor, nearest-neighbor
estimation, the curse of dimensionality, parametric models (linear, splines),
model selection trade-offs, the bias-variance decomposition, and classification
with KNN and the Bayes classifier.
