# Course 3 — Visualization & Math Bridge (Matplotlib/Seaborn & SciPy)

**Duration:** 1.5 hours
**Why this matters:** Visualizing the math demystifies what "training a model"
actually means. By the end of this session students have watched an optimizer
crawl down a 1-D loss surface — the entire mental model of supervised learning,
in three lines of SciPy.

## Schedule

| Time         | Topic                                     |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------|
| 0:00 – 0:45  | Matplotlib & Seaborn                      |
| 0:45 – 1:15  | SciPy for stats and optimization          |
| 1:15 – 1:30  | Grand Finale lab                          |

## Files

- `slides/index.html` — Reveal.js deck.
- `lecture.ipynb` — loss-curve plots, correlation heatmap, and `minimize`
  demo, followed by three exercises (each with its solution). The third
  exercise is the 15-minute **Grand Finale** capstone tying together Pandas,
  NumPy, and Matplotlib.

## Learning outcomes

1. Produce a loss-curve plot and a correlation heatmap without thinking.
2. Sample from a SciPy distribution and fit one back to data.
3. Use `scipy.optimize.minimize` to find the argmin of a 1-D function and
   articulate why this is "what a model does when it learns."
4. Complete the Grand Finale in ≤15 minutes: load → clean → transform → plot.
