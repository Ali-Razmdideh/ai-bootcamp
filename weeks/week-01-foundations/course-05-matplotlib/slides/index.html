Part 1 · 0:00 – 0:30

The Figure / Axes model

Three nouns

  • Figure — the page.
  • Axes — one rectangular plot area on the page.
  • Artist — anything you draw: line, marker, text, legend.

Every method follows from these three. fig.savefig, ax.plot, ax.legend — the prefix tells you who owns the action.

Your first plot, the right way

fig, ax = plt.subplots()
ax.plot(x, x**2, label='y = x²')
ax.set_xlabel('x'); ax.set_ylabel('y')
ax.set_title('Square function')
ax.grid(True); ax.legend()

Notice: no plt. calls after subplots(). Everything goes through ax (or fig).

Scatter — colored by a category


codes = penguins['species'].astype('category').cat.codes
ax.scatter(x, y, c=codes, cmap='viridis', s=30, alpha=0.7)

    
Pass an array to c= and a categorical colormap to cmap=. alpha tames overplotting.

Subplots & saving

  


  

    
A 2×2 grid in one call


fig, axes = plt.subplots(2, 2, figsize=(9, 6),
                         sharex=True)
axes[0, 0].plot(x, x);   axes[0, 0].set_title('x')
axes[0, 1].plot(x, x**2);axes[0, 1].set_title('x²')
fig.suptitle('Powers of x')
fig.tight_layout()

    
axes is a 2-D NumPy array of Axes. sharex=True aligns the x-axes across the grid.

Saving to PNG


fig.savefig('out.png', dpi=120, bbox_inches='tight')

    
Default DPI is too low for slides. Always pass dpi=120 or higher and bbox_inches='tight' to trim whitespace.

  


  

    
Style sheets — change the look in one line


with plt.style.context('seaborn-v0_8-whitegrid'):
    fig, ax = plt.subplots(); ax.plot(x, y)

    
Built-in styles: ggplot, dark_background, seaborn-v0_8-*. Switch globally with plt.style.use(...).
